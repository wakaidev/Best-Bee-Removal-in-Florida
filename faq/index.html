Frequently Asked Questions

Can bees be relocated?
It depends on the situation the bees are located in. Most of the time it is possible. Occasionally the bees must be destroyed. Registered Beekeepers follow the procedures made by “The Bureau of Plant & Apiary Inspection FDACS in order to relocate colonies.
Is it legal to kill and destroy bee colonies?
Yes, if the bees are wild and on your property. Although it is highly discouraged to attempt to remove bees yourself. Our technicians are highly trained to remove bees and they don’t mind getting stung either so you don’t have to.
What do I do if I am stung?
If you are having an allergic reaction to a bee sting. Call 911 immediately. For general information about bee stings, you can call the Florida Poison Control Center at 1-800-222-1222 or at their website https://floridapoisoncontrol.org/contact/contact-us/ You can remove stingers by scraping it out with a fingernail or credit card. Squeezing the stinger will release more venom.
Where should I check for Bees?
You should work to eliminate potential nesting sites. Walls, corners, and eaves of structures. Look for bees in work areas before using power equipment such as weed eaters, lawnmowers and chainsaws. Noise excites bees.
What should I do if I am being chased by Bees?
If you’re being chased by bees run away in a straight line, cover your face, most importantly your nose and mouth. Get inside a building or vehicle. Some bees may get in but it’s better than the swarm outside.
What is the difference between a swarm of bees and a colony of bees?
Part of the reproductive cycle of bees is called Swarming. Swarming is an event where a new colony is produced from an existing colony. The Swarm is a group of bees consisting of a new queen and many worker bees. The swarm can last anywhere from a few hours to a few days. They are looking for somewhere to start building their permanent residence known as a nest. Swarms tend to be less hostile than bees in a colony. Swarms are more interested in finding a home. While colonies prioritize protecting the nest at all costs.
What do I do if a find a Swarm or Colony on my property?
It is the responsibility of the property owner to deal with an unwanted swarm or colony. You have two options as a property owner. Remove the bees alive or have them exterminated. A registered Florida beekeeper can attempt to remove an established colony for you. This can include bees, honeycomb, brood, honey, etc. Never attempt to remove or exterminate bees or a colony yourself. What is the difference between Africanized and European Honey Bees?
It can be hard to tell the difference between Africanized honey bees from European honey bees based on physical appearance. Africanized bees tend to be more aggressive and slightly smaller than their European counterparts. A lab test would have to be done by a licensed beekeeper. Luckily, you don’t have to worry.
