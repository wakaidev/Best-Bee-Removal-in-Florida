Bees Bees Bees

12/04/2019

Did you know the honeybee is not native to North America? You mean someone CHOSE to bring bees into the US?

Honeybees have a long-standing relationship with humans - providing honey, beeswax, and both commercial and wild pollination. They are a natural pollinator, providing a great benefit to food supply crops and local agriculture. Most honeybees in the United States are maintained in artificial hives by professional beekeepers.

Honeybees will only swarm about once a year. A swarm occurs when the old queen leaves the hive with a portion of the hive. Those that stay behind work with a new recently born queen. A future queen is fed a special diet by the worker bees, making her fertile. The other female larvae are fed a regular diet which renders them infertile. The worker bees are those sterile female bees that didn’t make the “Queen Bee” cut. Male drone bees stay in the hive live a short life, residing in the hive during times of reproduction and are then killed by worker bees. It is rare that the bee you would see bumbling by in your yard is a honeybee, and even more rare that they would stop to sting you. That a bee can only sting you once is true... of honeybees - wasps, carpenter bees, and yellow jackets are another story.
